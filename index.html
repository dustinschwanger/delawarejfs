  • Child & Adult Protective Services

    Emergency: To report child abuse or neglect, call 740-833-2340 (24/7). For adult abuse (age 60+), call 740-833-2340 or toll-free 1-855-OHIOAPS (1-855-644-6277).

    Child Protective Services

    Mission: To ensure every child in Delaware County is in a safe and stable home. We serve youth who are, or may be at risk of abuse or neglect.

    How to Report: Call Delaware County Children Services at 740-833-2340 (available 24/7).

    Response Times:

    • Emergencies: Workers dispatched within 1 hour
    • Non-emergencies: Workers dispatched within 72 hours

    Confidentiality: The law protects the confidentiality of reporters. A social worker cannot reveal, confirm, or deny who a reporter may be. There are no consequences for reports made in good faith, even if unfounded.

    Adult Protective Services

    Adult Protective Services staff investigate reports of abuse, neglect (including self-neglect), or financial exploitation of adults age 60 and over who are unable to protect themselves due to serious physical or mental limitations.

    To Report Elder Abuse:

    • Local: 740-833-2340
    • Statewide: 1-855-OHIOAPS (1-855-644-6277)
    • Available 24/7

    Types of Elder Abuse:

    • Neglect: Caretakers failing to provide food, shelter, healthcare, or protection
    • Self-Neglect: Elderly individuals unable or unwilling to provide necessary self-care
    • Exploitation: Unauthorized use or theft of an elder's funds or property
    • Emotional Abuse: Verbal threats, intimidation, name-calling, harassment, and isolation
    • Physical Abuse: Injury inflicted through hitting, kicking, or weaponry
    • Sexual Abuse: Nonconsensual or forced sexual contact

    Foster Care & Adoption

    Who Can Become a Foster Parent? Single or married, homeowners or renters, age 21+. Licensing with ongoing training and criminal checks required.

    Support for Foster Families:

    • Monthly stipends based on child's age
    • Clothing allowances
    • Medical cards for foster children
    • Counseling services and support groups
    • Respite care
    • Case management

    Placement Information: Delaware County typically places no more than three foster children per home, with exceptions for sibling groups or emergencies. Placement duration ranges from overnight to approximately two years.

    Contact: 740-833-2300 for foster care and adoption information.

    State Resource: FosterAndAdopt.jfs.ohio.gov

    Safe Haven for Newborns

    Birth parents may leave an infant (up to 30 days old) with a medical worker at a hospital, emergency medical worker, or law enforcement officer without prosecution if the infant has not been abused.
